# The-Castel-Protocol

I built this because I was tired of horror mods that just throw a scary model at you and call it a day. The Castel Protocol isn't about jump scares; it’s about breaking your sense of safety. It's a physiological system designed to simulate a slow mental breakdown, where the game world you know stops following its own rules and starts actively trying to unnerve you.

How it Works

This mod introduces a system that tracks your "stability." As this stability drops, the world shifts around you. It doesn't just spawn a monster; it guts your foundation.

Environmental Corruption: You’ll see things that shouldn't be there. Blocks might momentarily appear "glitched," trees might lose their leaves silently, or you'll find passive animals facing walls, staring. It’s subtle enough to make you doubt what you just saw.

The Phantom System: It injects sounds directly into your audio stream—the sound of footsteps that stop when you stop, a chest opening in a nearby room when you're alone, or a very faint whisper using your own username.

The Unseeing: A new entity type that is completely invisible and silent, until your stability is critical. They always exist, moving around you, but their only interaction is collision. You'll run into "invisible walls" in open fields. You’ll feel something push you slightly. They are always right there.

Performance Warning

This is a heavy mod. The "The Unseeing" system constantly tracks player collisions, and the environmental corruption shader effects require a modern GPU. This is NOT optimized for weak hardware.

[!CAUTION] If you are experiencing major stuttering, you must configure your video settings. I highly recommend installing Embeddium or Sodium (depending on your loader) and tweaking the entity culling and chunk update settings. If your system can't handle it, the mod will not perform as intended.

Advice for Survival

If you feel like something is following you, it probably is. The worst thing you can do is acknowledge it. Keep moving, don't stay in the dark, and for the love of god, don't look behind you.
